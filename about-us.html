About the Rotary Club of Bath

Discover our mission, history, and how we make a difference in Bath and beyond.

Our Mission: Service Above Self

The Rotary Club of Bath is part of a global movement dedicated to creating lasting change.

Rotary International: A Global Force for Good

Rotary International's mission is to "provide service to others, promote integrity, and advance world understanding, goodwill, and peace through the fellowship of business, professional, and community leaders". As a non-political and non-religious organization, Rotary unites over 1.2 million members worldwide who volunteer approximately 47 million hours annually to address critical global challenges.

Our global causes include promoting peace, fighting disease (with a significant focus on polio eradication), providing clean water and sanitation, saving mothers and children, supporting education and literacy, fostering local economies, and protecting the environment.

The Rotary Club of Bath: Local Action, Global Vision

The Rotary Club of Bath, UK, is comprised of "people from this historic City and its surrounding area who work together to help make Bath, and the wider world, a better place". We are officially registered as a charity with the Charity Commission (Charity number: 267664), reflecting our commitment to transparent and impactful service.

Our local activities are diverse, ranging from organizing events to raise funds for various charities to undertaking major welfare and humanitarian projects in conjunction with Rotary International. We also provide direct support to charities by offering our members' diverse skills, such as working on projects with schools and colleges. A recent example of our impact includes raising much-needed funds for young carers in Bath and North East Somerset, providing them with crucial support and opportunities for personal development, including a Youth Leadership Awards program.

Our History and Evolution

Rooted in a rich tradition of service, the Rotary Club of Bath continues to adapt and grow. Our club was founded in 1921, celebrating its centenary in 2021. Below are key milestones in the history of Rotary in the UK, including our own club's journey.

1912

Rotary Arrives in Europe

The Rotary Club of London becomes the first Rotary club chartered in Europe, marking Rotary's intercontinental expansion.

1913-1914

Growth Across Great Britain and Ireland

Several more Rotary clubs are chartered across England, Ireland, and Scotland, including Glasgow, Belfast, Dublin, Edinburgh, Manchester, Liverpool, and Birmingham.

1914

Formation of BARC

The British Association of Rotary Clubs (BARC) is created in London to enhance camaraderie, unity, and standardize practices among UK Rotary clubs.

1921

Rotary Club of Bath Founded

The Rotary Club of Bath, UK, is officially founded, joining the growing network of clubs dedicated to service across Great Britain and Ireland.

2021

Centenary Celebration

The Rotary Club of Bath proudly celebrates 100 years of dedicated service to the local community and contributions to Rotary's global initiatives.

Present Day

Evolution to RIBI

BARC evolves into Rotary International in Great Britain and Ireland (RIBI), which remains the only territorial unit within Rotary International today, fostering a strong network of clubs across the UK and Ireland.

We embody Rotary's enduring motto of "Service Above Self," continually striving to meet the evolving needs of our local community and contribute to Rotary's global humanitarian efforts. Our commitment to service, fellowship, diversity, integrity, and leadership remains at the heart of everything we do.

We are an open organization, welcoming members from diverse backgrounds, genders, ethnicities, occupations, and ages, all united by a desire to make a positive impact.
